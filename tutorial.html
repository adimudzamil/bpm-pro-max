📘 Complete User Guide & Manual Updated July 23, 2026

🚀 What is BPM-Pro?

BPM-Pro is a powerful in-flight tool designed to quickly turn a BPM (Passenger Manifest) into a readable, printable, and interactive report. It helps cabin crew instantly identify special meals, Platinum/EMER tier passengers, and track in-flight meal orders.

Note on Development: Right now, only sc-737.html (737 Business Class) supports the advanced Interactive Order/Edit/Color popup system. A330 and A350 popup charts are currently in development and will receive the same features in an upcoming update.

📋 Part 1: Generating Your First Report

1

📱 Copy the Passenger Manifest

On Windows/Mac (PC):

  1. Open BPM and go to your flight.
  2. Click anywhere in the passenger list.
  3. Press Ctrl+A (or Cmd+A on Mac) to select everything.
  4. Press Ctrl+C (or Cmd+C) to copy.

On Android Phones:

  1. CRITICAL: Install the "XClipper" app from the Play Store first. Android has a copy limit that XClipper bypasses.
  2. Open BPM, tap and hold on the word "FLT NBR".
  3. Select all text using the handles, then use XClipper to copy.

On iPhone/iOS:

  1. Open BPM in Landscape Mode (turn your phone sideways).
  2. Tap and hold on "FLT NBR". Drag the blue handles down to select everything down to "Showing X entries".
  3. Tap "Copy".
2

📋 Paste the Manifest

  1. Back on BPM-Pro, click the main textbox that says "Paste passenger manifest here...".
  2. Press Ctrl+V (or Cmd+V) to paste. On phones, tap-and-hold and select "Paste".
3

⚙️ Generate the Report

  1. Click the blue "Generate Report" button.
  2. OR, press Ctrl+Enter (or Cmd+Enter) on your keyboard for instant generation.
  3. The tool will auto-detect your aircraft type and layout. The report will appear below.

🖥️ Part 2: The Main Interface (Advanced Settings)

Below the text box, you'll find the Advanced Settings panel. Click the button to expand it. Here is what every button and slider does:

✈️ Aircraft & Layout

  • Aircraft Type: Select the specific plane (737NG, 737MAX, 737NR, A330-300, etc.) or choose "Auto-detect" to let the tool figure it out.
  • Custom Configuration: If your aircraft isn't listed, you can manually create custom zones (e.g., "Zone A: Rows 1-10").
  • Layout Style:
    • Single: Standard column list.
    • Port/Starboard: Splits the list into left and right sides.
    • Seat Chart: A visual block grid of seats.
    • Seat Chart (Business): Opens a 737/A330 business class visual chart.
    • Dual Chart (737): See specific guide below.

🎨 Visual Controls

  • Font Size: Controls the size of the main list (7px to 14px).
  • Summary Text Size: Controls the size of the Summary boxes (9px to 14px).
  • Table Width: Shrinks or stretches the report to fit your printer margins (50% to 150%).
  • Seat Chart Font Size: Controls the text size specifically inside the graphical seat grid.
  • Aft Perspective View: If checked, the seat chart reverses rows and columns (great for printing and flipping the paper to view from the back of the cabin).
  • Show All Business Class: If unchecked, only Business Class passengers with special meals appear. Check this to show EVERY Business Class passenger.

🖨️ Print Layout Options

  • Compact Print Mode: Saves 20-30% paper on large flights (100+ passengers).
  • Landscape Orientation: Rotates the print to fit wide tables and large fonts.
  • Page Break Between Zones: Starts every zone (e.g., B, C, D) on a new printed page.
  • Avoid Orphan Rows: Keeps at least 2 rows together at page breaks.
  • Show Unassigned in Print: Includes the list of passengers with no assigned seat in the printed copy.
  • Print Scale & Margins: Customize the scale (50% to 150%) and set specific millimeter margins (top, bottom, left, right) for your printer.

📊 Part 3: Navigating the Generated Report

  • Report Tabs: If you generate multiple flights, tabs will appear at the top of the report. Click them to switch between different flights without losing data.
  • Summary Bar: A sticky bar at the top showing total counts for Special Codes, INF (infants), EXT (extension seatbelts), and REMARKS. Clicking a chip opens a modal to view the specific passengers in that category.
  • Flight Info Bar: Click the blue "Flight Info" bar at the top to open the Seat Lookup modal. It features an on-screen keypad allowing you to quickly input a seat number (e.g., 3A) and instantly see the passenger's details or edit their remarks/special codes.
  • Meal & Icons: In the list view, PLAT members have a ❤️ icon, EMER members have a 🔴 icon, and passengers celebrating a birthday have a 🎂 icon next to their meal.

🔄 Part 4: Dual Chart Mode (737 Business Class)

This is for generating both First Service and Second Service manifests on a single printed page.

  1. Toggle "Dual Chart Mode ON".
  2. Ensure your Layout is set to "Dual Service Chart - 737 Business Class".
  3. Paste your FIRST SERVICE manifest and click "Generate Report".
  4. A new popup window will open with the first chart. Wait for the "Chart ready" message.
  5. Back in the main window, clear the text box. Paste your SECOND SERVICE manifest.
  6. Click "Generate Report" again. It will send the second manifest to the existing popup.
  7. Print the popup. Both services will be printed together on one page.

🎛️ Part 5: The 737 Interactive Seat Chart (Popup)

⚠️ Fleet Compatibility Note:
Right now, only sc-737.html (737 Business Class) features the advanced Color, Edit, and Order system described below. A330, A350, and other fleet seat charts are currently in development and will be updated to match these features in a future version. (Updated: July 23, 2026)

When you generate a Seat Chart report for 737 Business Class, it opens in a new popup window. This popup has 4 powerful modes accessible via the top control panel:

1. View Mode

Default mode. This is a static, read-only view of the passenger manifest in a visual seat grid. You see the passenger titles, surnames, tier badges (*PLAT* / *EMER*), and special meals.

2. 🎨 Colours Mode

Tap on any colored part of the seat grid to customize it perfectly for your flight.

  • Tap the Tier Badge: Opens a color picker to change the background and text color of that specific tier (e.g., PLAT, EMER).
  • Tap the Meal Code: Changes the background color of a specific meal code (e.g., VGML, SPML).
  • Tap the Seat Background: Changes the cell background color, border color, and empty seat placeholder color.
  • Backup/Restore: Use the buttons in the control panel to save your color theme to a JSON file, or restore a saved theme.

3. ✏️ Edit Mode

Tap any seat cell to edit the passenger's details.

  • You can change their Title, Surname, First Name, Meal Code, Tier, and toggle Infant (INF) or Extension Seatbelt (EXT).
  • You can also add Remarks and assign a Special Handling code (e.g., DEPO, MAAS, MEDA) or enter a custom code.
  • Swap Seats: While in Edit mode, click the "Swap" button in the control bar. Tap seat A, then tap seat B to instantly swap their passenger data.

4. 🍽️ Order Mode (The New In-Flight Meal Tracker)

This mode transforms the chart into an interactive serving tool. Pro-Tip: Use a stylus or your finger on a tablet for the best experience.

Setting up the Meal Menu:

  • Click the "🍽️ Meal Menu" button in the control bar.
  • Service Name: You can now select preset service names (Breakfast, Brunch, Lunch, Dinner, All Day Dining, Supper, Refreshments, DAT) from a dropdown, or choose "Custom" to type your own name.
  • Add Meal Choices: Click "+ Add meal choice".
    • Quick Presets: Click the Name input box. A popup appears with preset buttons: Chicken, Beef, Fish, Seafood, Prawn, Vegetarian. Tap one to instantly insert the name.
    • Numeric Keypad: Click the Quantity box. A full touchscreen numeric keypad pops up for fast, accurate quantity entry (0-9, Backspace, Done).

Taking Orders & Serving:

Once the menu is set, tap a passenger's seat cell.

  • The Overlay: The seat cell fills with the chosen meal's background color. A massive, easy-to-read first letter of the meal (scaled with your font size) appears in the center.
  • Remarks Input: A bold, clearly visible text box sits beneath the letter. You can type special notes (e.g., "Extra sauce") here. Clicking the input box does NOT trigger the "served" checkmark – it allows free typing.
  • The Seat Number: The seat number stays in the top-left corner. Clicking the Seat Number toggles the passenger's status between Served (✅) and Not Served (First Letter).
  • The "Peek" Feature: If you need to double-check the passenger's name or original details, tap any empty background space inside the colored overlay. The overlay instantly disappears, revealing the full passenger info beneath. Tap the empty cell again to bring the serving overlay right back.

✨ Pro-Tips & Best Practices

  • Keyboard Shortcut: Generate the report instantly using Ctrl+Enter (or Cmd+Enter on Mac) after pasting.
  • Paper Saver: For flights with 100+ passengers, Compact Print Mode shrinks tables and removes extra spacing to save 20-30% paper.
  • Missing Rows: If the seat grid looks incorrect, double-check your Aircraft selection in Advanced Settings. For example, if you have Row 4 Business passengers, select 737NR.
  • Offline Mode: Click "Download Offline Version" next to the main text box. This saves the entire tool as a standalone HTML file that works perfectly without internet.
  • Persistent Themes: The colors you set in the 737 popup are automatically saved in your browser's localStorage. If you close and reopen the popup, your custom colors and font settings are restored.

❓ Still Stuck? Common Troubleshooting

  • "Copy fails on Android": You must have XClipper installed. Android has a strict text limit without it.
  • "Report shows blank business class seats": Business seats are deliberately left blank for you to write in standard meals (Chicken/Beef/Fish).
  • "Print cuts off on the right": In your browser's print window, change "Scale" to "Fit to page".
  • "Colors disappear when printing": In your print settings, make sure "Print background colors and images" is checked.
  • "I tapped the background and lost the overlay!": Don't worry! This is the Peek feature. Just tap the empty cell again, and your serving overlay (and typed remarks) will come right back.
